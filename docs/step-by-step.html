Hexagonal Bicupola Wind Rotor — Assembly Instructions
Read before starting Read all steps before cutting any rods. The belt rod (F5) length is sensitive — cut one and dry-fit it before cutting all twelve.
No T-to-L stays T connects only to U nodes. B connects only to L nodes. Any rod from T directly to L is a mistake.
Count your rods The finished frame has exactly 36 rods. If you have more or fewer after assembly, find the error before adding sails.
Shaft alignment critical Top and bottom bearings must be exactly coaxial. Misalignment causes vibration under load. Use a jig or spirit level.

Assembly Steps
1
Lay out the node diagram. Before cutting anything, draw or print the top-down plan. Label T at centre. Place U1 at 0°, then U2 at 60°, U3 at 120°, U4 at 180°, U5 at 240°, U6 at 300°. Confirm you have six evenly spaced positions.
DIAGRAM ONLY
ℹ️  No parts needed yet. This step is verification only. A mistake here propagates to every rod and hub.
2
Add the lower ring (L) to the diagram, staggered 30°. L1 sits at 30°, L2 at 90°, L3 at 150°, L4 at 210°, L5 at 270°, L6 at 330°. Each L node falls exactly between two adjacent U nodes. Confirm: L1 sits between U1 and U2.
DIAGRAM ONLY
⚠️  The 30° stagger is not approximate. It is exact. This offset drives the belt edge connectivity in step 5.
3
Calculate your three rod lengths. Choose your target dimensions: outer ring radius r, vertical distance from equator to apex h, and vertical distance from equator to ring d. Then calculate:

F1 = F2 = √(r² + (h−d)²)   fan rods (12 total)
F3 = F4 = r   ring rods — equal to ring radius (12 total)
F5   belt rods — calculate from 3D coordinates of adjacent U and L nodes (12 total)
CALCULATION
ℹ️  For a first prototype, a practical starting point is r = 400mm, h = 500mm, d = 80mm. Write down all three lengths before cutting anything.
⚠️  Cut ONE of each family first. Dry-fit against a hub before cutting the rest.
4
3D print and test Hub U. Hub U is degree 5 — five rods enter it. Print one Hub U. Insert one F1, two F3, and two F5 rods into the appropriate sockets. All five rods must sit at the correct angles without forcing. If the rods bind or the hub cracks, adjust the hub geometry before printing all six.
HUB U ×1 F1 ×1 F3 ×2 F5 ×2
🛑  Do not print all 12 hubs until this single-unit test passes. Hub angle errors are expensive and slow to correct.
5
Assemble the top fan. Mount Hub T on the shaft at the top position (do not fix permanently yet). Insert six F1 rods into Hub T. Attach one Hub U to the end of each F1 rod. Connect adjacent U hubs with six F3 ring rods to close the hexagon. The result is a six-spoked star with a hexagonal rim.
HUB T ×1 HUB U ×6 F1 ×6 F3 ×6
ℹ️  The U hexagon must close exactly. If the last F3 rod does not fit without forcing, the F3 length is wrong. Recheck before continuing.
6
Attach belt rods from U ring downward. From each U hub, run two F5 belt rods diagonally downward — one to the L node clockwise of it, one to the L node counter-clockwise of it. Following the belt table:

U1 → L6 and L1  ·  U2 → L1 and L2  ·  U3 → L2 and L3
U4 → L3 and L4  ·  U5 → L4 and L5  ·  U6 → L5 and L6

Insert Hub L at the lower end of each belt rod pair.
HUB L ×6 F5 ×12
🛑  Belt connectivity is the most error-prone step. After attaching all 12 belt rods, verify the belt table above before proceeding. Each L hub should have exactly two belt rods entering it from above.
7
Close the lower ring and bottom fan. Connect adjacent L hubs with six F4 ring rods (same length as F3 — cut from the same batch). Then insert six F2 fan rods from each L hub downward, converging at Hub B. Mount Hub B on the shaft at the bottom.
HUB B ×1 F4 ×6 F2 ×6
ℹ️  Total rod count at this point: 6+6+12+6+6 = 36. Count before proceeding.
⚠️  Do not fix T or B hubs permanently until you have checked for twist and verified the frame is symmetric when viewed from above.
8
Mount shaft and bearings. The shaft must extend beyond T and B by enough to seat both bearings in the support structure. Mount the top bearing (radial load only) in the upper bearing housing. Mount the bottom bearing (radial and axial/thrust load) in the lower housing. The rotor frame rotates with the shaft. The shaft rotates inside the fixed bearing housings.
SHAFT ×1 BEARING ×2 SUPPORT STRUCTURE
🛑  Both bearing housings must be exactly coaxial. Check alignment with a spirit level or dial gauge before fixing. Misalignment causes vibration under load and will damage bearings rapidly.
9
Attach upper sails (from T). Cut three triangular sail panels from ripstop nylon. Each panel covers one triangle: T–U1–L1, T–U3–L3, T–U5–L5. Hem all edges. Add grommets at each corner. Attach corners to T hub, the relevant U hub, and the relevant L hub using lacing cord or zip ties. The diagonal edge from T down to the L node is sail fabric — it is not attached to any stay rod.
UPPER SAIL ×3 GROMMETS LACING CORD
ℹ️  Upper sails occupy sectors 1, 3, 5. Sectors 2, 4, 6 remain open frame at this stage.
10
Attach lower sails (from B). Cut three more triangular sail panels. Each covers one triangle: B–L1–U2, B–L3–U4, B–L5–U6. These fill the alternating sectors left open by the upper sails. The diagonal edge from B up to the U node is sail fabric only — it is not a stay rod.
LOWER SAIL ×3 GROMMETS LACING CORD
ℹ️  After this step all six sail sectors are filled — alternating blue (upper) and green (lower) around the rotor. Six sectors remain open frame between sails.
11
Mount the generator on the shaft below B. The generator mounts on the shaft extension below the bottom bearing. The bottom bearing carries both radial and axial (thrust) load. The generator must be a low-RPM permanent magnet type — this is a drag-based rotor with tip-speed ratios below 1.0 and typical operating speeds of 50–200 RPM. If using a high-speed generator, fit a step-up gearbox between the bearing and the generator.
GENERATOR ×1 SHAFT COUPLING
⚠️  Do not use a generator designed for wind turbine blade tips (3000+ RPM) without a gearbox. It will produce negligible voltage at rotor operating speeds.
12
Balance and test.

1. Allow the rotor to rest on the shaft. The heavy side will sink. Add small stainless balancing weights to light sectors until the rotor rests at any angle without rotating under gravity alone.

2. Spin by hand. It should rotate smoothly without wobble.

3. Expose to low wind first. Confirm counter-clockwise rotation when viewed from above (wind from the front). If it spins the wrong way, the sail orientation is reversed — recheck sail attachment.

4. Connect generator output to a load (resistor or battery via charge controller). Measure output voltage. If output is negligible at operating speed, the generator RPM rating is too high — fit a gearbox.
BALANCING WEIGHTS MULTIMETER
ℹ️  Low cut-in speed (the wind speed at which rotation begins) is the most important performance metric for a small drag rotor. If cut-in is too high, consider adding paired pocket sails spanning two adjacent triangles per sector to increase drag area.
