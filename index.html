Physics note — rotation direction

This is a drag-based Savonius-style vertical-axis wind turbine. Each sail is a triangular panel spanning from an apex (T or B) diagonally across to the opposite ring. Wind pressure on the open concave face of each sail pocket drives that face away from the wind source. The closed triangular frame faces on the alternating sectors present far less drag area and return with low resistance.

With wind arriving from the front (toward the viewer), the rotor spins counter-clockwise when viewed from above — that is, the open sail pockets on the front hemisphere retreat to the right, and the closed frame sectors advance from the left. The animation reflects this direction. If your physical shaft rotation appears reversed when tested outdoors, reverse the generator winding connections rather than the rotor geometry.

Graph structure

Vertex order top to bottom: T (top apex) → U1–U6 (upper ring) → L1–L6 (lower ring) → B (bottom apex).

Edges (36): 6 top fan (T–U), 6 upper ring, 12 staggered belt, 6 lower ring, 6 bottom fan (B–L). All 24 faces triangular.

Sails (6, alternating): Upper from T: T–U1–L1, T–U3–L3, T–U5–L5. Lower from B: B–L1–U2, B–L3–U4, B–L5–U6. Dashed colored lines are plastic sail edges, not fiberglass stays.
